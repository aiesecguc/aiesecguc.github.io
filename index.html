AIESEC in GUC

AIESEC is an international a nongovernmental not for profit organizations that provides young people with leadership development and cross-cultural global internship and volunteer exchange experiences across the globe, with a focus to empower young people so they can make a positive impact on society.

Global Community
Development Program

Global Community development program is an internship hosted by an AIESEC entity under a project or an organization e.g. NGO, organizations or schools in another country or territory. A GCDP internship ranges from 6 weeks to 8 weeks.

Host Family Program

Host families is a program established by AIESEC GUC where you and your family can explore new cultures, widen your perspective to the world, have a multicultural experience and expand your relations internationally. AIESEC is non-for-profit organization, so money is not involved in this program. We do this because we seek to learn from the different ways of life and opinions represented in our multicultural environment. We do this because living diversity is one of our values in AIESEC.

WHERE TO NEXT?

Nowhere, We will bring the world home to you. Being a host family is a great opportunity for you and your family to explore new cultures, widen your perspective to the world, have a multicultural experience and expand your relations internationally. It also satisfy one of our six values in AIESEC which is living diversity.

AIESEC gave me this amazing opportunity to develop my leadership potential and clear the clouds of dilemma. Now I am more confident and have a clear vision regarding what I am and what I want to be. you want to go to exchange choose a country in which you never want to be born and live your fantasy that would give you the experience of your life .I feel proud that I made the right choice. Egypt as a country taught me how to be solution oriented in my toughest time. Everything seems to be a dream at first. Everyday going into the streets of an Arabic country with a contrasting culture wasn't an easy job but in the end I came to a simple conclusion that what you hear is not what you see. The people in Egypt are very humble and courteous with a much disciplined lifestyle. It's fascinating to see that how even after so much globalisation and upheavals a country is able to preserve its culture and inheritance .it's not easy to survive in a city with recent history of riots but this thrill gave me the experience I really wanted. There was time when a bomb blast 5km away was as fascinating as a firework show .I went for exploring Egypt but I end up exploring myself. Such is a beauty of this country. I really want to thank AIESEC in GUC. These 42 days which you gave me saved the next 42 years of my life.
